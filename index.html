A Gentleman Scholar

Born on this day in 1959 in St. Albans, England, Robin Wheatley has cultivated a life of remarkable depth and distinction. His passion for the Great Western Railway speaks to an appreciation for the golden age of engineering and design.

A devotee of classical music — strictly composed before 1900 — Robin's tastes reflect a profound understanding of the masters: Bach, Mozart, Beethoven, Wagner, Brahms. His discernment extends to the visual arts, where he favors passionate, detailed works over the abstract and conceptual.

His extensive travels across German, Italian, and English landscapes, including years residing near Berlin's Brandenburg Gate and the serene beauty of Lake Constance, have shaped a worldview both cosmopolitan and deeply rooted in European cultural traditions.

Robin's appreciation for British television classics — from "All Creatures Great and Small" to "Downton Abbey," from the wit of "Fawlty Towers" to the brilliance of "Monty Python" — reveals a man who values both sophistication and humor, tradition and innovation.

Today we celebrate not merely a birthday, but a life lived with conviction, knowledge, and an uncompromising standard of excellence.
